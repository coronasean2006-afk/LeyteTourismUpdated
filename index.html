About the Province

History, coast, highlands, and everyday welcome.

Location

Leyte is in Eastern Visayas, Philippines. It faces Leyte Gulf and sits near Samar, Cebu, Bohol, and Southern Leyte, making it a natural gateway across the Visayas.

Background

The province is deeply tied to Philippine history, especially the Leyte Gulf landings of October 20, 1944. Its towns also preserve older Visayan, Spanish-era, and Waray traditions.

Known Attractions

Travelers come for Kalanggaman Island, Cuatro Islas, Lake Danao, San Juanico Bridge, MacArthur Landing Memorial, Canigao Island, mountain trails, and heritage churches.

Culture and Traditions

Leyte celebrates faith, resilience, music, street dance, food craft, and community pride through Pintados-Kasadyaan, Sangyaw, town fiestas, and pasalubong-making traditions.

Why Visit

One itinerary can hold island hopping, war history, slow lake mornings, and food stops.

Leyte works beautifully for travelers who want variety without losing a sense of place. You can wake up by a sandbar, spend the afternoon at a memorial or bridge viewpoint, and end the day with binagol, moron, and a quiet coastal sunset.

Festivals and Culture

Color, faith, and stories in motion.

Tacloban City and Leyte Province | June

Pintados-Kasadyaan Festival

A major cultural-religious celebration inspired by the tattooed Pintados warriors and the joy of Kasadyaan. Delegations from Leyte towns perform stories of faith, folklore, harvest, and local identity.

Learn More

Tacloban City | June

Sangyaw Festival

Sangyaw means to proclaim or announce. Tacloban's festival honors the Santo Nino with street dancing, vivid costumes, floats, music, and a citywide expression of pride and devotion.

Learn More

Dagami | May

Dinagamihan Festival

A town celebration that highlights Dagami's local products, community heritage, and the pride behind beloved Leyte delicacies such as binagol, moron, and sagmani.

Food and Delicacies

Sweet, sticky, coconut-rich, and made for pasalubong.

Binagol

A sweet taro and coconut dessert steamed inside a polished coconut shell and wrapped in banana leaves.

Where to try it: Dagami stalls, Tacloban pasalubong centers Learn More

Moron

A chocolate-flavored sticky rice roll cooked with coconut milk, usually wrapped in banana leaves.

Where to try it: Tacloban, Palo, Dagami Learn More

Sagmani

A soft native suman made with cassava, gabi, or sweet potato, enriched with coconut cream and sugar.

Roscas

Travel Tips

Small choices that make Leyte easier to enjoy.

Best Time to Visit

Plan many beach and island trips from March to May for sunnier weather. Always check local forecasts because Eastern Visayas can receive rain in any season.

Getting Around

Use Tacloban or Ormoc as convenient bases. Vans, buses, jeepneys, tricycles, boats, and hired transport connect most towns and island jump-off points.

Book Island Trips Early

For Kalanggaman, Cuatro Islas, and Canigao, coordinate with local tourism offices or boat operators ahead of time, especially on weekends and holidays.

Bring Cash

Many smaller towns, ports, and food stalls still prefer cash. Keep smaller bills for entrance fees, boat shares, tricycle rides, and snacks.

Safety Reminders

Wear life vests on boats, follow local swimming boundaries, hire guides for hikes or caves, and avoid risky travel during heavy rain or rough seas.

Respect Local Places

Ask before photographing people, dress modestly in churches and memorials, bring reusable bottles, and take trash back from beaches and trails.
