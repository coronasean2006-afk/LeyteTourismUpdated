A vibrant June celebration of faith, folklore, street dance, and Leyte identity.

History and Character

The story of Pintados-Kasadyaan Festival

Pintados-Kasadyaan combines two powerful cultural ideas: the tattooed Pintados warriors described in early Visayan history and kasadyaan, or merriment. The festival gathers delegations from Leyte towns to perform stories of faith, folklore, harvest, and community pride.

Interesting Facts

  • The festival is usually celebrated in June.
  • Performances often use body-paint motifs inspired by precolonial Visayan tattoo traditions.
  • The event highlights both provincial culture and Catholic devotion.

What to Do

Watch street dancing, photograph costumes respectfully, attend cultural programs, and sample festival food.
