Red Beach

A coastal landscape where beach scenery and World War II memory meet.

History and Character

The story of Red Beach

Red Beach is historically important because it was part of the landing area used by Allied forces in October 1944. Today, its shoreline is often visited together with the MacArthur Landing Memorial.

Interesting Facts

  • The beach name is tied to military landing zones.
  • Its historical value makes it different from Leyte's island-hopping beaches.
  • It helps visitors understand why Leyte is central to Philippine World War II memory.

What to Do

Walk the shore, photograph the coast, visit nearby memorials, and reflect on wartime history.
