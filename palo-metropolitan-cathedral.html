History and Character

The story of Palo Metropolitan Cathedral

The Palo Metropolitan Cathedral, formally the Metropolitan Cathedral of Our Lord's Transfiguration, traces its roots to the early mission history of Palo. It became a cathedral in 1938, served as a hospital during World War II, and later became a symbol of faith and recovery after Typhoon Yolanda damaged the area in 2013.

Interesting Facts

  • The National Historical Commission marker notes Palo's mission history beginning in 1596.
  • The cathedral became the mother church of the Archdiocese of Palo.
  • Pope Francis visited Palo during his 2015 trip to typhoon-affected communities.

What to Do

Visit for prayer, view the facade and interior, photograph heritage details respectfully, and pair the stop with nearby Palo historical sites.
